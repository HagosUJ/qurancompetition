International Qur'anic Competition 2025

Join us for Nigeria's maiden International Qur'anic Recitation Competition, uniting 44 participants from 20 countries to celebrate the Holy Qur'an.

A Global Celebration of Qur'anic Excellence

Explore the
Event Schedule
View details

Nigeria hosts its first-ever International Qur'anic Recitation Competition in August 2025.

Spearheaded by Hon. Muhammad Adam Alkali and supported by the Centre for Islamic Studies at Usman Danfodio University, Sokoto, this landmark event will feature 44 male and female participants from 20 countries, including Cameroon, Ghana, Chad, Senegal, and Saudi Arabia. The competition aims to promote Islamic values, foster unity, and showcase excellence in Qur'anic recitation, memorization, and understanding.

Starting in Jos, Plateau State, and concluding in Abuja, the event will bring together global Islamic communities under the guidance of six esteemed judges, celebrating the Holy Qur'an as the ultimate guide for a meaningful life.

Event Schedule

Mark your calendars for this historic Qur'anic recitation competition.

  • Arrival in Abuja.

    International participants and officials arrive in Abuja on August 20th and 21st, 2025.

  • Abuja Committee Setup.

    Reception arrangements begin with the arrival of the Abuja Committee on August 20th, 2025.

  • Travel to Jos.

    Participants depart from Abuja to Jos on August 22nd, 2025.

  • Opening of Musabaqa.

    The competition runs from August 23rd to 26th, 2025 (29th Safar, 1446), in Jos, featuring recitations and evaluations by six judges.

  • Return to Abuja.

    Participants travel back to Abuja on August 27th, 2025.

  • Closing Ceremony.

    The grand finale and prize distribution take place in Abuja on August 30th, 2025.

  • Departure.

    International participants depart on August 31st and September 1st, 2025.

Participants & Countries

A diverse gathering of 44 reciters from 20 nations.

The competition will feature 44 male and female participants from countries including Cameroon, Ghana, Chad, Senegal, Kenya, Tanzania, Mauritania, Niger, Egypt, Morocco, Libya, Algeria, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Qatar, Malaysia, England, the UAE, and the USA. Each country will send talented reciters and officials, showcasing the global richness of Qur'anic scholarship.
